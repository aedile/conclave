# **Business Requirements Document: Air-Gapped Synthetic Data Generation Engine**

## **Executive Summary**

In the contemporary enterprise landscape, the proliferation of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and complex algorithmic decision-making has fundamentally altered the paradigm of data consumption. Organizations across all sectors recognize that high-quality, diverse, and voluminous datasets are the lifeblood of innovation, particularly for the development of agentic AI systems capable of autonomous reasoning and action.1 However, a profound tension exists between this insatiable demand for data and the increasingly draconian global regulatory environment governing data privacy, including frameworks such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).3 Production data—the most accurate representation of user behavior and systemic interactions—is inherently messy, highly interconnected, and fraught with sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Protected Health Information (PHI).6 Exposing this raw, unvarnished data to development, testing, or AI training environments introduces unacceptable regulatory, financial, and reputational risks.6

Historically, organizations have relied on elementary data masking, redaction, and substitution techniques to sanitize data for lower environments.6 While these subtractive methods provide a baseline level of privacy, they often destroy the underlying statistical utility, referential integrity, and complex relational logic required for rigorous software testing and advanced model training.8 Furthermore, for enterprises operating in defense, intelligence, healthcare, and critical infrastructure, the risk extends beyond regulatory compliance to national security and absolute data sovereignty.10 These highly regulated entities mandate absolute zero-trust, zero-exfiltration architectures, often deploying critical systems within physically isolated, air-gapped networks completely disconnected from the broader internet.10 Traditional Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) or fully managed cloud data platforms are categorically disqualified in these environments due to their reliance on external control planes and internet-based telemetries.12

To resolve this intractable dilemma, the business requires the development of an enterprise-grade, mid-tier Air-Gapped Synthetic Data Generation Engine. This platform is envisioned as a versatile, highly secure utility designed to achieve feature parity with industry leaders such as Tonic.ai, MOSTLY AI, and Syntho, but engineered fundamentally from the ground up for environments requiring absolute isolation.14 Operating on a strict Bring Your Own Compute (BYOC) deployment model, the software will empower customers to deploy the engine entirely within their own virtual private clouds (VPCs) or on physically disconnected on-premise hardware.12 This architecture ensures that the data plane and the control plane reside exclusively within the customer's perimeter, eliminating the risk of vendor-initiated access or data exfiltration.12 The platform must scale elastically, functioning efficiently on a single user's multi-node home server rack for localized AI experimentation, while possessing the capability to orchestrate terabyte-scale synthesis across a fully isolated enterprise cluster.19

The core value proposition of this platform is its ability to transform production data into privacy-safe synthetic replicas that preserve the complex statistical patterns, referential integrity, and business logic of the original datasets without retaining any actual sensitive values.20 By synthesizing data rather than merely masking it, the business can overcome severe data scarcity challenges, generate theoretical edge cases that do not yet exist in production, and fuel complex agentic AI models safely.1 The engine will serve as a multifaceted "Swiss Army knife," providing targeted subsetting and deterministic masking for rapid functional testing, alongside advanced generative AI synthesis for deep model training.8

Crucially, the platform must transition the organization from heuristic anonymization to mathematical certainty. It will integrate formal differential privacy mechanisms, providing quantifiable, mathematically provable guarantees that no reverse-engineering of the output or differencing attacks can expose an individual's identity.23 This is augmented by rigorous compliance tracking, continuous immutable auditing via inline compliance preparation, and adherence to cryptographic erasure standards defined by NIST SP 800-88, ensuring that temporary data remnants are permanently destroyed.26

This Business Requirements Document outlines the comprehensive functional and non-functional capabilities required to deliver this vision. The product's design, development, and operational features are strictly governed by the provided "Developer Constitution".28 Under this mandate, security is explicitly defined as Priority Zero, superseding all other functional considerations.28 The platform will enforce unbreakable quality gates, mandate strict accessibility compliance according to Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 AA and Section 508 standards, and provide robust, architecturally ingrained mitigation against the OWASP Top 10 security vulnerabilities.28 By executing this vision, the business will deliver a definitive solution that enables the most secure organizations in the world to safely unlock the latent value of their siloed data, accelerating the software development lifecycle and AI innovation while maintaining an impenetrable, audit-ready security posture.

## **User Personas & Stories**

The Air-Gapped Synthetic Data Generation Engine must serve as a highly versatile, multi-disciplinary utility tailored to the distinct operational realities and pain points of three core user personas. Each persona faces unique friction points when operating within restricted, zero-trust environments, and the platform's business logic must intelligently adapt to accommodate their specific, sometimes conflicting, workflows. The success of the platform hinges on delivering a seamless user experience (championed by Priority 9 of the Developer Constitution) that natively balances the Data Scientist's need for statistical fidelity, the QA Engineer's need for structural speed, and the Compliance Officer's demand for absolute, provable security.8

### **Persona Profiles and Contextual Realities**

**Persona 1: The Data Scientist & AI Researcher** The Data Scientist is tasked with building complex, predictive machine learning models, natural language processing algorithms, and autonomous agentic AI systems within a highly secure, disconnected environment.1 Their primary pain point is severe data scarcity. Because organizational security policies strictly prohibit the movement of raw production data into modeling environments, they are often forced to rely on elementary, rule-based mock data.1 This mock data lacks the statistical nuance, latent correlations, and distributional complexity required to train sophisticated models effectively.21 Consequently, models trained on mock data fail to generalize when exposed to real-world scenarios. Furthermore, the Data Scientist needs the ability to simulate rare, edge-case events—such as complex fraudulent transactions, rare disease indicators, or highly specific multi-turn conversational intents—that are statistically improbable or insufficiently represented in the original production data.1 They require high-fidelity, mathematically robust synthetic datasets that mirror the exact behavior of real users without containing a single byte of actual personal information.21

**Persona 2: The QA & Software Engineer** The Quality Assurance (QA) and Software Engineer is responsible for rapid functional testing, system integration, load testing, and ensuring the stability of continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipelines.20 Their operational reality is defined by the need for speed and structural validity. Production databases are vastly too large and far too sensitive to duplicate into local development or ephemeral test environments.20 Existing data masking techniques often break application logic because they fail to maintain referential integrity; for instance, if a customer ID is masked in a primary user table, but the corresponding foreign keys in a disparate billing table are not updated to match, the application will crash during testing.20 The QA Engineer does not necessarily require the deep statistical nuance demanded by the Data Scientist; rather, they need the ability to rapidly extract a small, referentially intact "slice" (subset) of a massive database, apply deterministic, format-preserving masking rules to the sensitive fields, and provision this structurally valid "junk" data instantly.8

**Persona 3: The Compliance & Security Officer** The Compliance & Security Officer manages organizational risk, enforces zero-trust architecture, and ensures strict adherence to global privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA) and federal security mandates (e.g., NIST, OWASP).10 Operating in an air-gapped environment, they face compounded challenges. They cannot rely on cloud-native security posture management tools or automated over-the-air updates.10 Their primary pain point is the inability to verify the safety of AI-generated data through visual inspection alone; they must possess mathematical certainty that the generated data cannot be reverse-engineered to re-identify an individual (differencing attacks).24 They require strict, centralized control over the platform, demanding role-based access controls (RBAC), immutable audit logs (inline compliance prep) that track every system interaction, and mechanisms for the cryptographic erasure of temporary data.26 They view the platform not just as a data tool, but as a critical compliance enforcement mechanism.

## **Functional Requirements**

This section details the comprehensive business capabilities and workflow logic the Air-Gapped Synthetic Data Generation Engine must possess to fulfill the epics outlined above. The requirements are constructed to be entirely agnostic of the underlying technology stack, focusing exclusively on business logic, operational execution, and domain capabilities required to manage data securely in a zero-trust environment.

### **Data Agnosticism and Connectivity Integration**

To function as a ubiquitous, vendor-neutral utility, the platform must seamlessly interface with diverse, heterogeneous data structures without enforcing vendor lock-in, requiring massive architectural redesigns, or relying on external cloud APIs for data mapping.48 The platform must be the centralized hub where fragmented data sources are understood and synthesized.48

* **Multi-Modal Data Ingestion and Profiling:** The engine must support the localized ingestion and rapid profiling of both structured tabular data (e.g., relational databases) and unstructured text data.44 Upon connection, the platform must automatically infer database schemas, ascertain data types, and determine semantic meaning without requiring laborious manual intervention. For example, the system must recognize that a specific column, regardless of its arbitrary naming convention, contains physical addresses, financial identifiers, or healthcare codes, automatically suggesting the appropriate synthesis or masking rules.44  
* **Relational Integrity and Virtual Foreign Key Mapping:** Enterprise data is rarely confined to a single flat file; it is distributed across highly complex, multi-table schemas. The system must possess the capability to automatically detect and map both explicit database constraints and implicit (virtual) foreign key relationships.20 This capability is absolutely critical. It ensures that when a primary customer record is synthesized or masked, all associated transactional data in child tables (e.g., order history, support tickets) remains correctly and consistently linked to the newly generated synthetic identity, preserving the holistic business logic.20  
* **Broad Input/Output (I/O) Protocol Support:** The platform must read from and write to standard enterprise data formats (such as CSV, JSON, and Parquet) and connect to common relational and non-relational datastores via generic, vendor-agnostic connection protocols (e.g., standard ODBC/JDBC equivalents).48 Crucially, all data ingestion and egress must operate strictly within the confines of the local area network or VPC, ensuring no data ever traverses an external gateway.13

### **Subsetting and Deterministic Masking Workflows**

For QA and software engineering personas focused on CI/CD pipeline integration, the platform must provide rapid data minimization and precise de-identification capabilities, prioritizing speed and structural validity over deep statistical modeling.3

* **Targeted, Relationally Intact Subsetting:** Users must be empowered to define custom business rules (e.g., "Extract all customer records where the status is active and the geographic region is the European Union") or simple percentage-based targets (e.g., "Extract exactly 5% of the total database volume").22 Following this input, the platform's engine must mathematically calculate the necessary relational pathways across all mapped tables to extract the minimum viable dataset required to satisfy the request. The resulting subset must strictly preserve all foreign key constraints, ensuring that no "orphaned" records are generated that would cause downstream application failures.22  
* **Format-Preserving Masking and Substitution:** The platform must offer an extensive, pre-built library of deterministic masking, redaction, substitution, and tokenization algorithms.6 The masking must be intelligent and format-preserving. If a source field contains a valid credit card number, the masked output must not only be structurally identical (maintaining the correct string length and prefix) but must also successfully pass the Luhn algorithm validation check.3 This ensures that when the "junk" data is fed into an application during functional testing, it bypasses basic input validation logic without exposing real financial data.  
* **Enterprise-Wide Deterministic Consistency:** The engine must guarantee consistent masking across entirely distinct, physically separated databases within the air-gapped environment. If the same real customer email address exists in both a legacy CRM system and a modern billing database, the platform must synthesize it into the exact same fake email address in both target outputs.16 This deterministic consistency is vital for integration testing across microservices that rely on shared unique identifiers.

### **Generative AI Modeling and Statistical Synthesis**

For Data Scientists requiring highly nuanced data for machine learning model training, the platform must transition from simple rule-based obfuscation to advanced, AI-driven synthesis.21 The goal is to generate data that captures the latent patterns of reality without memorizing it.

* **AI-Generated Synthesis (Sample-Based Learning):** The engine must ingest a real-world sample dataset and deploy advanced generative models to learn the inherent statistical properties, multi-variate correlations, and probability distributions of the original data.21 Once trained, the engine must generate an entirely distinct, statistically identical dataset from scratch.21 The generated records must be completely fabricated; they cannot be a one-to-one mapping or encrypted version of the original PII. The utility of this data must be measurable, ensuring that models trained on the synthetic output perform with high parity to those trained on raw data.5  
* **Rule-Based and Greenfield Mock Data Generation:** In scenarios where no production data exists—such as greenfield application development or the introduction of entirely new features—the platform must allow users to generate structurally valid mock data from scratch. This is achieved using predefined templates, customizable business rules, and bounded random value generators, providing realistic data shapes before any actual user data is collected.8  
* **Conditional Generation and Anomaly Amplification:** To improve the robustness of predictive models, the system must permit users to conditionally skew the synthetic generation process. Users must be able to over-sample rare events or minority classes (e.g., artificially increasing the frequency of network intrusion alerts, fraudulent banking transactions, or rare disease indicators).5 This provides data scientists with diverse, voluminous training examples for edge cases that are critically important but statistically scarce in the real-world seed data.

### **Mathematical Privacy Guarantees (Differential Privacy)**

Traditional anonymization techniques—such as simply removing names or masking direct identifiers—are fundamentally insufficient against modern "differencing attacks." In these attacks, an adversary utilizes overlapping auxiliary datasets to reverse-engineer and re-identify individuals within a supposedly anonymous dataset.9 To satisfy the Compliance Officer persona, the platform must integrate formal, mathematically provable privacy controls.

* **Differential Privacy Engine and Noise Injection:** The platform must inject a precisely calibrated amount of statistical noise into the data generation pipeline.24 This mathematical mechanism ensures that the presence, or complete absence, of any single individual's record in the source data does not significantly alter the probability distribution of the synthetic output.23 By ensuring the output remains statistically indistinguishable whether a specific person is included or not, the platform provides a provable guarantee against re-identification.  
* **Epsilon Privacy Budget Management:** The system must provide a highly privileged configuration interface for Compliance Officers to define the global "privacy budget," denoted by the mathematical parameter epsilon (ε).24 This budget quantifies the acceptable level of privacy loss. A lower epsilon value mandates the injection of more statistical noise, resulting in higher privacy but lower data utility. Conversely, a higher epsilon value injects less noise, yielding higher utility at the cost of reduced privacy.45 The platform must allow the business to explicitly define this trade-off.  
* **Budget Depletion and Refresh Workflows:** Differential privacy is a finite resource. The platform must meticulously track the cumulative consumption of the epsilon budget across multiple queries or synthesis runs targeting the same underlying seed data.35 Every time the data is accessed to generate a new synthetic model, a portion of the budget is depleted. Once the budget is fully exhausted, the system must categorically block any further generation requests against that specific dataset.35 Generation can only resume when an authorized administrator explicitly executes a budget refresh workflow, preventing continuous, iterative querying from slowly stripping away the privacy protections.35  
* **Privacy vs. Utility Trade-off Visualization:** To aid in decision-making, the user interface must display clear, quantifiable metrics demonstrating the inverse relationship between the selected epsilon value and the expected utility (e.g., predictive accuracy, feature importance preservation) of the resulting synthetic dataset.4 This allows business stakeholders to balance regulatory compliance with operational data needs effectively.

### **Offline Licensing and Air-Gapped Operations**

Given the absolute zero-trust environment, the software must function perpetually and reliably without requiring internet connectivity, vendor "call-home" telemetry, or dynamic cloud-based license verification.10

* **QR Code and JWT License Activation Protocol:** The platform must provide a highly secure, frictionless offline license activation mechanism.46 When deployed on the air-gapped network, the software must generate a cryptographic challenge specific to that installation. This challenge is represented visually as a QR code. An administrator can scan this QR code with an external, internet-connected mobile device to access the vendor's licensing portal. The portal then generates a secure JSON Web Token (JWT).46 This JWT is transferred back to the air-gapped machine—either via manual string entry or via an approved physical USB transfer—to verify the license locally and unlock the platform's functionality.46  
* **Hardware Node-Locking:** To protect the vendor's intellectual property and prevent unauthorized proliferation of the software within the customer's network, the offline license must be strictly node-locked. The JWT must inextricably tie the software's execution rights to the specific hardware configuration and localized identifiers of the authorized host machine or cluster within the VPC.46 If the software is illicitly copied to another server, the validation must fail.

### **Cryptographic Erasure and Data Sanitization**

In air-gapped environments, the physical replacement or destruction of hard drives is operationally burdensome and expensive.11 Therefore, to mitigate the risk of sensitive data remnants lingering after a generation task, the platform must natively support rigorous, software-based data destruction protocols.11

* **NIST SP 800-88 Compliant Erasure Workflows:** When temporary seed data, intermediate generative models, or cached subsets are deleted from the platform, the system must execute formal data sanitization workflows fully compliant with the NIST SP 800-88 guidelines.26 The choice of erasure method must be configurable based on the data's classification level.  
* **Logical Clear and Cryptographic Purge Operations:**  
  * *Clear:* For standard operations, the system must support logical erasure by overwriting user-addressable storage areas with predefined, random binary patterns, ensuring standard forensic recovery is impossible.26  
  * *Purge (Cryptographic Erasure):* For highly sensitive workloads (e.g., military intelligence or PHI), the platform must facilitate cryptographic erasure. By generating encrypted working volumes and subsequently permanently destroying the associated encryption keys, the platform renders the encrypted temporary data instantly and irreversibly unrecoverable, even against sophisticated, state-level forensic analysis.26  
* **Tamper-Proof Sanitization Reporting:** Upon the successful completion of an erasure protocol, the system must automatically generate a certified, offline report confirming the sanitization event. This tamper-proof log serves as critical evidence for internal audits and external regulatory inspections.53

### **Audit and Compliance Logging (Inline Compliance Prep)**

In isolated networks, organizations cannot rely on external, cloud-native security posture management or monitoring tools.10 The platform must be entirely self-auditing, providing continuous visibility into system usage without exposing data.

* **Continuous Immutable Auditing (Inline Compliance Prep):** The platform must implement an architectural paradigm known as "Inline Compliance Prep." This mechanism automatically wraps every human interaction, API call, and automated system command with structured, provable compliance metadata.27 The system must immutably record who executed a synthesis run, the specific masking rules or epsilon parameters applied, the exact data source accessed, and crucially, whether any queries were blocked due to privacy policy violations.27 This eliminates the need for manual screenshotting or post-hoc log aggregation.  
* **Offline Dashboarding and Anomaly Detection:** All generated audit logs must be immediately accessible via a localized, visually intuitive dashboard operating entirely within the air gap.41 This interface allows compliance officers to review real-time risk metrics, track the depletion of privacy budgets, export formatted reports for SOC 2 or HIPAA audits, and identify access anomalies or suspicious usage patterns without ever extracting data from the secure perimeter.41

## **Non-Functional Requirements**

Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs) define the systemic attributes of the platform, such as its security posture, accessibility, engineering quality, and performance limitations. For this specific product, the NFRs are not merely engineering guidelines; they are strict business mandates dictated by zero-trust architectural principles, federal accessibility laws, and the prioritized rules established in the Developer Constitution.28 Failure to meet these NFRs constitutes a failure of the product.

### **Security and Zero-Trust Architecture (Priority 0 Mandate)**

As explicitly mandated by the Developer Constitution, Security is Priority Zero.28 The platform must operate under a foundational "Security First" paradigm. The system is strictly prohibited from executing any action, defaulting to any configuration, or providing any user pathway that could result in data leaks, infrastructure exposure, or unauthorized access.28

#### **Bring Your Own Cloud (BYOC) Isolation Enforcement**

* **Complete Perimeter Isolation:** The platform must operate strictly within the customer's defined boundary—whether that is a single local server or a complex enterprise VPC cluster.12 The architecture must feature a localized control plane and data plane that never attempt to initiate outbound network connections to the vendor or any third-party service.12 This preserves absolute data sovereignty.  
* **Cryptographic Encryption Everywhere:** All internal communications between the various micro-components of the platform operating within the air-gapped network must be encrypted using strong cryptographic standards (TLS 1.2 or higher). This internal encryption prevents lateral data interception in the event that a malicious actor breaches the physical perimeter of the network.18

#### **OWASP Top 10 Mitigation as Business Logic**

The platform's underlying business logic and system architecture must inherently mitigate the critical security risks defined by the OWASP Top 10 standard.29

| OWASP Vulnerability | Required Business Mitigation Strategy |
| :---- | :---- |
| **A01: Broken Access Control** | The system must enforce strict, granular Role-Based Access Control (RBAC). The business logic must ensure that users (e.g., QA Engineers) cannot bypass UI flows to access raw, unmasked seed datasets that are intended exclusively for backend AI ingestion algorithms or highly privileged Data Scientists.56 |
| **A02: Cryptographic Failures** | The platform must mandate encryption at rest and in transit by default. The business must automate cryptographic key rotation and enforce the aforementioned NIST Purge (cryptographic erasure) protocols for the management of the data lifecycle.54 |
| **A03: Injection** | The system must implement rigorous input validation and data sanitization protocols for all user-submitted masking rules, custom database queries, and generative AI prompts. | **A04: Insecure Design** | The platform must mandate a "Privacy-by-Design" approach. All default system settings must prioritize maximum data obfuscation and minimum privilege. Users must explicitly opt-in to reduce privacy protections (e.g., manually increasing the epsilon budget).32 |
| **A05: Security Misconfiguration** | The software must perform automated baseline security checks upon initialization. It must disable web server directory listings, ensure no default administrative accounts are active, and verify that no unnecessary network ports are exposed within the local environment.56 |

### **Accessibility and Usability (Priority 9 Mandate)**

User-facing design must champion the end-user experience, adhering to Priority 9 of the Developer Constitution.28 The platform must strictly comply with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA and Section 508 (Bobby 504\) standards.30 This is not merely a design preference; it is a legal imperative for platforms deployed within federal agencies or federally funded institutions, mitigating the risk of ADA-related litigation.39

The user interface must be architected around the WCAG POUR principles:

* **Perceivable:** Information and UI components must be presented in ways all users can perceive. The platform must provide descriptive, highly meaningful alternative (alt) text for all system-generated charts, graphs, and utility metrics (e.g., the differential privacy utility trade-off visualizations).30 Furthermore, the UI must ensure a minimum color contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for standard text and 3:1 for large text and interface components, ensuring that data visualizations never rely solely on color to convey critical information.30  
* **Operable:** The platform must be fully functional without the use of a mouse. The entire interface—including complex configuration workflows for masking rules, epsilon budget adjustments, and synthesis triggers—must support comprehensive, logical keyboard navigation.30 Additionally, the system must provide users with sufficient time to read instructions and complete complex tasks without imposing arbitrary or unexpected timeouts.59  
* **Understandable:** Information and the operation of the UI must be highly predictable. The system must provide clear, context-aware error messages. For example, if a differential privacy budget refresh fails, the system must explicitly state the reason and offer actionable suggestions for correction, rather than presenting a generic error code.30  
* **Robust:** The UI must be reliably interpreted by a wide variety of assistive technologies, including offline screen readers utilized by visually impaired personnel operating within the secure facility.30

To support enterprise procurement processes, the business must maintain an updated Voluntary Product Accessibility Template (VPAT) that comprehensively documents the platform's conformance with these WCAG 2.1 AA and Section 508 standards.61

### **Engineering Quality Gates and Standards (Priorities 1-8 Mandate)**

To guarantee the reliability, security, and maintainability of the platform, the product delivery lifecycle must incorporate unbreakable quality gates as defined by the Developer Constitution.28 While these represent engineering practices, they are strictly enforced business requirements for the product's ultimate acceptance.

* **Automated Security Tooling (Priority 1):** The product must never be shipped or deployed unless it has successfully passed rigorous automated secret scanning, security linting, and structural checks. Any failure in these automated gates must definitively halt the delivery pipeline.28  
* **Test-Driven Development & Coverage (Priorities 3 & 4):** All functional capabilities outlined in this document must be validated by a robust, comprehensive test suite achieving a minimum of **90% test coverage**. No feature is considered complete until all unit and integration tests pass, ensuring zero regressions when new generative algorithms or masking rules are introduced to the platform.28  
* **Meticulous Documentation (Priority 6):** The platform must be shipped with comprehensive, offline-accessible documentation. This includes highly detailed system logs, decision architecture records, and user manuals formatted for clarity, ensuring that administrators can troubleshoot and operate the system entirely without vendor support.28

### **Performance and Compute Scalability**

Operating within a BYOC paradigm requires extreme flexibility in resource utilization. The software cannot assume the availability of infinite cloud compute.12

* **Elastic Compute Scalability:** The software architecture must efficiently scale horizontally. It must be capable of executing lightweight, deterministic masking rules on a single-node home server rack for localized AI experimentation, while seamlessly scaling its orchestration to utilize an entire isolated enterprise cluster (e.g., on-premise Kubernetes) for parallel, terabyte-scale data synthesis.19  
* **Processing Optimization:** Complex generative algorithms (such as deep neural networks utilized for sample-based synthesis) and differential privacy noise injection mechanisms are highly computationally expensive.5 The platform must optimize memory allocation and natively support hardware acceleration (e.g., interfacing with localized GPU clusters) within the air-gapped environment. This optimization is critical to meeting the targeted Time-to-Data KPIs, ensuring that synthetic generation does not become a new operational bottleneck.4

## **Assumptions & Dependencies**

The successful deployment, secure operation, and realized business value of the Air-Gapped Synthetic Data Generation Engine rely on several critical assumptions regarding the customer's operational environment and internal processes. If these dependencies are not met, the platform's efficacy and security posture may be severely compromised.

### **Business and Operational Assumptions**

1. **Hardware Provisioning and Infrastructure Capacity:** Because the platform operates on a strict BYOC model, the vendor provides only the software application. It is fundamentally assumed that customers operating air-gapped networks possess the necessary on-premise computing resources—including sufficient CPU/GPU processing power, RAM, and dedicated storage arrays—to support the rigorous mathematical processing required for differential privacy and generative AI synthesis.4 Insufficient localized hardware will directly result in a failure to meet operational KPIs.  
2. **Internal Domain Expertise and Risk Tolerance:** While the platform's user interface is designed to be highly accessible and understandable, configuring advanced parameters—specifically the epsilon privacy budget—requires a foundational understanding of data science, statistics, and organizational privacy risk tolerance.24 It is assumed that customer organizations have trained Compliance Officers and data stewards capable of defining appropriate business risk thresholds and understanding the profound trade-offs between mathematical privacy and data utility.4  
3. **Physical Security and Insider Threat Mitigation:** Air-gapping isolates networks from digital, external cyber threats, but it inevitably concentrates risk on internal personnel. The architecture shifts the threat vector to insiders—administrators, operators, and maintenance staff with physical access to the isolated terminals.10 It is assumed that the customer enforces rigorous physical security protocols, continuous localized monitoring, and strict background vetting of all personnel allowed to access the air-gapped environment. The software cannot protect against a malicious actor with physical access to the server hardware.10

### **Platform and Workflow Dependencies**

1. **Offline Media Transfer Policies:** The initial deployment, subsequent patching, and offline license activation of the software fundamentally depend on the customer's internal security policies regarding the use of physical media. Data must periodically enter and leave the environment.62 It is assumed that the organization has secure, audited procedures for utilizing encrypted USB drives, optical media, or specific unidirectional gateways to transfer the required JWT license tokens or software updates across the air gap.11 If an organization strictly prohibits all physical media ingress, offline license activation must rely entirely on the manual string entry of the QR code response.47  
2. **Seed Data Quality and Inherent Bias:** The quality, accuracy, and fairness of AI-generated synthetic data are inherently dependent on the quality of the raw production seed data.1 Generative AI models learn and replicate the patterns they are fed. If the original production data contains inherent demographic biases, significant missing values, or systemic errors, the platform's algorithms will accurately synthesize, replicate, and potentially amplify those flaws.1 The business assumes that the customer's data engineers will perform initial quality control, bias detection, and sanitization on the seed datasets prior to initiating the synthesis process.1  
3. **Active Directory (AD) Availability for Access Control:** To fulfill the stringent OWASP mitigation requirements for identification and authentication, the platform relies on robust access controls. It is assumed that the customer maintains an active, correctly configured localized Active Directory (AD) server operating strictly within the air-gapped perimeter.42 The presence of this localized AD infrastructure is critical for the platform to enforce advanced Role-Based Access Control, localized Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), and contextual login policies (e.g., restricting access based on time-of-day or specific localized IP addresses) without attempting to reach the external internet.42 Without this dependency, the system must fall back to less secure, localized, application-level credential management.
